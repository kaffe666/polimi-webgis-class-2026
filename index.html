Overview

About This Project

This project analyses air quality conditions in Greece for three pollutants — NO₂, PM2.5 and PM10 — and presents the results through an interactive WebGIS platform. The study integrates pollutant concentration data, land cover change, population distribution and administrative boundaries to assess both spatial pollution patterns and population exposure.

01

Data Collection

The analysis uses CAMS reanalysis pollutant rasters, ESRI Land Cover data, WorldPop population data and GAUL Level 2 administrative boundaries. Together these datasets support pollutant mapping, land cover transition analysis and exposure assessment for all three pollutants.

02

Spatial Analysis

Raster clipping, reprojection, annual aggregation, reclassification, raster calculation and zonal statistics were used to process the air quality and land cover datasets, converting raw spatial data into interpretable maps and charts.

03

Exposure Assessment

Bivariate mapping combines pollutant concentration classes with population classes. This method identifies areas where high pollution levels overlap with dense population, indicating higher exposure risk for residents.

Datasets and Processing Environment

The project combines satellite-derived pollutant data, land cover products, population grids and administrative boundaries. QGIS was used for spatial processing and layout design, while GeoServer and OpenLayers deliver the published layers to the WebGIS viewer.

CAMS Reanalysis

Monthly pollutant rasters used to map NO₂, PM2.5 and PM10 concentration patterns.

ESRI Land Cover

Land cover rasters from 2021 and 2023 used to detect built area and land cover transitions.

WorldPop

Population raster data used to evaluate population exposure to pollutant concentration classes.

GAUL Level 2

Administrative boundaries used for zonal statistics and bivariate exposure mapping.

QGIS

Main GIS platform for clipping, reprojection, raster calculation, classification and map layouts.

GeoServer + OpenLayers

GeoServer publishes the WMS services, while OpenLayers v7 builds the interactive WebGIS interface.

Key Findings

NO₂ concentrations are mainly concentrated around urban and transport-related areas, especially near Athens and parts of northern Greece. PM10 levels are highest in northern lowlands and areas of active construction, while PM2.5 is strongly tied to urban agglomerations such as the Athens basin. Across all three pollutants, the bivariate exposure analysis shows that population distribution strongly affects how air quality risk should be interpreted.
